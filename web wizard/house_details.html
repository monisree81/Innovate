History of Hogwarts

Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, commonly referred to as Hogwarts, is a magical school founded in the 10th century by four famous witches and wizards: Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin. The school is located in a remote area of Scotland and has educated some of the most famous witches and wizards of all time. Over the years, Hogwarts has grown to become the most prestigious magical institution in the wizarding world, with students sorted into four distinct houses, each representing different virtues and qualities.

Explore the Hogwarts Houses

Gryffindor

Founded by Godric Gryffindor, known for courage, bravery, and determination. Famous members include Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, and Ron Weasley.

Hufflepuff

Founded by Helga Hufflepuff, known for loyalty, patience, and fair play. Famous members include Cedric Diggory and Nymphadora Tonks.

Ravenclaw

Founded by Rowena Ravenclaw, known for intelligence, wisdom, and creativity. Famous members include Luna Lovegood and Cho Chang.

Slytherin

Founded by Salazar Slytherin, known for ambition, cunning, and resourcefulness. Famous members include Draco Malfoy and Severus Snape.
